What Airmed Does

Airmed models the dynamics of cellular aging through a unified framework of damage accumulation, propagation feedback, and capacity response—all with complete transparency and reproducibility.

01

COP-12 Input Framework

Twelve normalized oscillation primitives map to hallmarks of aging: genomic instability, telomere attrition, epigenetic drift, proteostasis, autophagy, mitochondrial function, nutrient sensing, senescence, SASP, stem cell exhaustion, intercellular noise, and inflammation.

02

D-R-C Dynamics

Three coupled state variables—Damage, propagation (Recursion), and Capacity—evolve through time according to transparent differential equations. Every parameter is exposed and adjustable.

03

CELL-PHASE-7 Classification

Deterministic phase assignment across seven states: Homeostasis, Drift, Insult, Propagation, Stasis, Compensation, and Collapse. Each phase has clear threshold conditions.

04

Paradox Gain Control

Explicit recursion/feedback pressure parameter that amplifies propagation dynamics. Model runaway conditions or stable states with a single auditable knob.

05

Governance Layer

Alexandria-style append-only ledger tracks all actions. Phase-based blocking prevents certain operations in high-recursion states. Override with acknowledged scars.

06

Full Export & Audit

Export complete simulation traces as JSON or CSV. Every run is reproducible. Built for researchers who need to show their work and regulatory environments that demand transparency.
